FINEFLOW KNOWLEDGE BASE — COMPLETE REFERENCE Last updated: May 2026 ===================================================== TOPIC: WHAT IS FINEFLOW / OVERVIEW / ABOUT ===================================================== Fine Flow is a UK-based automated fleet fine and PCN management platform. It handles the entire lifecycle of parking and penalty fines for fleet companies — from reading the fine email, to assigning responsibility to the correct driver, to managing appeals and logging outcomes — so the fleet manager does not have to do any of it manually. Mission: Turning penalties into progress. Core promise: Cut admin time by up to 80% and never miss a penalty deadline again. Fine Flow provides 24/7 fleet fine tracking, management, and compliance in one place. TOPIC: HOW FINEFLOW WORKS / FINE LIFECYCLE / PROCESS ===================================================== Fine Flow processes every fine through these stages: Stage 1 — Email Ingestion: Fine Flow monitors the connected Gmail inbox every 1 minute. When an email arrives with keywords like fine, penalty, violation or challan in the subject line, it is picked up automatically. Two connection methods: Gmail OAuth 2.0 (primary, recommended) and App Password IMAP (fallback). Stage 2 — Data Extraction: Fine Flow uses Groq LLaMA 3.1 AI to read the email and extract: fine amount and currency, citation number, type of violation, issue date and time, payment due date, location, and vehicle registration. The fine is created with status RECEIVED and one credit is deducted. Stage 3 — Driver Assignment: The system matches the fine to a driver using three criteria: vehicle registration must match exactly, fine issue date must be within plus or minus 1 day, and fine time must fall within the driver log start and end time. A clear match sets status to ASSIGNED. No match sets status to UNASSIGNED and places the fine in the admin review queue. Stage 4 — Driver Action: The assigned driver can confirm responsibility (status becomes CONFIRMED) or dispute the fine with a reason and optional evidence (status becomes DISPUTED). Stage 5 — Appeal Review: Admin reviews disputed fines. If accepted, Fine Flow generates an appeal letter and sends it to the issuing authority — status becomes UNDER REVIEW. If rejected, fine returns to CONFIRMED. Stage 6 — Outcome Tracking: Final statuses: PAID, CANCELLED (appeal won), or OVERDUE (deadline passed without resolution). Overdue Scheduler: Every day at midnight the system checks all fines past their due date. Any fine with status RECEIVED, UNASSIGNED, ASSIGNED or CONFIRMED that is past its due date becomes OVERDUE automatically. TOPIC: FINE STATUSES / STATUS MEANINGS ===================================================== RECEIVED — Fine ingested from email, data extracted, credit deducted. UNASSIGNED — Fine created but no driver matched yet, in admin review queue. ASSIGNED — Driver has been matched to the fine automatically. CONFIRMED — Driver has confirmed they were responsible. DISPUTED — Driver has raised a dispute and appeal record created. UNDER REVIEW — Appeal has been submitted to the issuing authority. PAID — Fine has been marked as paid. CANCELLED — Appeal was successful, fine is cleared. OVERDUE — Due date has passed without resolution. REVIEW_REQUIRED — Multiple driver matches found, admin must resolve. TOPIC: PAYMENT / DOES FINEFLOW PAY FINES / AUTOMATIC PAYMENT ===================================================== IMPORTANT: Fine Flow does NOT automatically pay fines. Fine Flow NEVER logs into authority websites to pay fines on your behalf. Payment portals used by government authorities and councils have anti-bot protection, session controls, and card verification requirements that prevent reliable automation. It would be unstable and unsafe to attempt this. What Fine Flow does is everything around the payment step: it captures the fine, extracts all details, assigns responsibility, tracks the deadline, flags urgency, and organises everything so the payment process is fast and controlled. When you are ready to pay, Fine Flow takes you directly to the correct authority page with everything already prepared. The actual payment is completed by you on the authority's website. TOPIC: APPEALS / DISPUTE / CHALLENGE A FINE / APPEAL MANAGEMENT ===================================================== Fine Flow manages appeals as a fully structured process: How appeals work: 1. Driver disputes a fine from their dashboard, providing a reason and optional evidence (JPG, PNG or PDF, up to 10MB). 2. Admin reviews the appeal and can accept or reject it. 3. If accepted: Fine Flow generates the appeal letter using the fine details and historical outcomes, and sends it directly to the issuing authority by email. Status becomes UNDER REVIEW. 4. If rejected: Fine returns to CONFIRMED status and the driver must pay. Appeal intelligence: Fine Flow does not treat each appeal in isolation. It tracks outcomes from every appeal submitted and learns what has been accepted or rejected before. Over time it improves its recommendations and generates stronger appeal letters based on what actually works. Win percentage: Each appeal has a win probability percentage shown before submission. This is a guidance signal based on past outcomes — not a guarantee. It helps you decide whether to submit before investing time in the appeal. TOPIC: DRIVER MATCHING / HOW DRIVERS ARE ASSIGNED / VEHICLE LOGS ===================================================== Fine Flow matches fines to drivers automatically using your uploaded driver logs. The matching uses three criteria: 1. Vehicle registration must match exactly. 2. Fine issue date must be within plus or minus 1 day of a driver log entry. 3. Fine time must fall within the driver's logged start and end time. If all three match: fine is ASSIGNED to that driver automatically. If no match: fine is UNASSIGNED and placed in the admin queue for manual review. If multiple drivers match: fine is flagged as REVIEW_REQUIRED for admin resolution. Driver logs are not mandatory. Without them, Fine Flow still captures and tracks all fines — it just means manual assignment is needed. The better your logs, the more Fine Flow can automate.
