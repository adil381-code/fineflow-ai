When an email arrives with keywords like fine, penalty, violation or challan in the subject line, the system picks it up automatically. Two connection methods are supported: Gmail OAuth 2.0 (primary) and App Password IMAP (fallback). STAGE 2 — DATA EXTRACTION The system uses Groq LLaMA 3.1 AI to read the email and extract nine key fields: 1. Fine amount and currency 2. Citation number (reference number / PCN number) 3. Type of violation 4. Issue date and time 5. Payment due date 6. Location of violation 7. Vehicle registration number Once extracted the fine is created in the system with status RECEIVED and one credit is deducted from the company's balance. STAGE 3 — DRIVER ASSIGNMENT The system attempts to automatically match the fine to a driver based on three criteria: 1. Vehicle registration must match exactly 2. Fine issue date must be within plus or minus 1 day of a driver log entry 3. Fine time must fall within the driver log start and end time If a match is found the fine status changes to ASSIGNED. If no match is found it remains UNASSIGNED and sits in the admin queue for manual review. If multiple matches conflict, the fine is flagged as REVIEW_REQUIRED for the admin to resolve. STAGE 4 — FINE STATUSES RECEIVED — Fine ingested from email, data extracted, credit deducted UNASSIGNED — Fine created but no driver matched yet ASSIGNED — Driver has been matched to the fine CONFIRMED — Driver has confirmed they were responsible DISPUTED — Driver has filed an appeal / dispute UNDER REVIEW — Appeal has been submitted to the issuing authority PAID — Fine has been marked as paid OVERDUE — Due date has passed without resolution CANCELLED — Appeal was successful, fine cancelled STAGE 5 — DRIVER ACTION Once assigned, the driver can: - Confirm responsibility — status moves to CONFIRMED - Dispute the fine — driver provides reason and optional evidence, status moves to DISPUTED, appeal record created STAGE 6 — APPEAL REVIEW When a driver disputes a fine, the company admin reviews it: - Accept the appeal — email sent to authority, status moves to UNDER REVIEW - Reject the appeal — fine moves back to CONFIRMED, driver must pay Evidence upload: JPG, PNG or PDF format, up to 10MB. Appeal outcomes are tracked and reported in the appeals dashboard. STAGE 7 — OVERDUE SCHEDULER Every day at midnight the system automatically checks all fines where the due date has passed. Any fine with status RECEIVED, UNASSIGNED, ASSIGNED or CONFIRMED that is past its due date is automatically updated to OVERDUE. PAID, CANCELLED and DISPUTED fines are not affected. ============================================================= SECTION 4: GMAIL CONNECTION ============================================================= METHOD 1: Gmail OAuth 2.0 (Primary / Recommended) - User clicks Connect Gmail in dashboard - Redirected to Google login, approves access - No password stored in Fine Flow - Tokens encrypted with AES-256-CBC - Tokens auto-refresh 5 minutes before expiry METHOD 2: App Password IMAP (Fallback) - User generates a 16-character app password inside Google account settings - Pastes it into Fine Flow - Password encrypted before storage The system checks Gmail OAuth first.
