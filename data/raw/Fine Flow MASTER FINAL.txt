TOPIC: HOW FINEFLOW WORKS / FINE LIFECYCLE / WHAT HAPPENS TO A FINE
============================================================
Once a fine enters the system, Fine Flow extracts the key details — including the amount, PCN or reference number, vehicle registration, issue date and location.
It then compares this against your driver data and logs (uploaded via CSV or entered in the system), using vehicle, date and time matching to identify who was responsible.
If a match is found, the fine is assigned automatically. If not, it's placed into an unassigned queue for quick manual review, so nothing gets missed or delayed.

Stage 1 — Email Ingestion: Fine Flow monitors the connected Gmail inbox every 1 minute. When a penalty email arrives it picks it up automatically. Two methods: Gmail OAuth 2.0 (recommended) and App Password IMAP (fallback). An admin can also manually upload fines if preferred.

Stage 2 — Data Extraction: Fine Flow uses AI to read the email and extract: fine amount, citation number, violation type, issue date and time, payment due date, location, and vehicle registration. The fine enters the system with status RECEIVED — this is when billing for that fine is triggered.

Stage 3 — Driver Assignment: The system matches the fine to a driver using three criteria: vehicle registration must match exactly, fine issue date must be within plus or minus 1 day, and fine time must fall within the driver log start and end time. A clear match sets status to ASSIGNED. No match sets status to UNASSIGNED.

Stage 4 — Driver Action: The assigned driver can confirm responsibility (status becomes CONFIRMED) or dispute the fine with a reason and optional evidence (status becomes DISPUTED).

Stage 5 — Appeal Review: Admin reviews disputed fines and can accept or reject.
If accepted: status becomes UNDER REVIEW and Fine Flow generates an appeal letter, sending it to the issuing authority via email.
If rejected: fine returns to CONFIRMED status.

Stage 6 — Outcome Tracking: Final statuses are PAID, CANCELLED (appeal won) or OVERDUE.

Every day at midnight the system checks all fines past their due date and marks them OVERDUE automatically.


TOPIC: FINE STATUSES
============================================================
RECEIVED — Fine ingested, data extracted, billing triggered.
UNASSIGNED — No driver matched yet, in admin review queue.
ASSIGNED — Driver matched automatically.
CONFIRMED — Driver confirmed responsibility.
DISPUTED — Driver raised a dispute, providing a reason and optional evidence.
UNDER REVIEW — Admin accepted the dispute; appeal letter generated and sent to the issuing authority.
PAID — Fine marked as paid.
CANCELLED — Appeal successful, fine cleared.
OVERDUE — Due date passed without resolution.
REVIEW_REQUIRED — Multiple driver matches found, admin must resolve.


TOPIC: PAYMENT / DOES FINEFLOW PAY FINES / AUTOMATIC PAYMENT
============================================================
No — and that's intentional, not a limitation.
Government and authority payment systems are heavily protected, with bot detection, session controls, and security layers that prevent reliable automation. Card verification requirements mean automation is blocked at a systems level. It would be unstable and risky to attempt to bypass this, so payments are always completed by you directly on the authority's site.

Fine Flow handles everything up to that step: capturing incoming fines automatically, extracting key details, assigning responsibility, tracking deadlines, flagging risk, and keeping everything organised. By the time you reach payment, there's no thinking left — just click the link and complete it on the authority's site.

Fine Flow does NOT log into council or authority websites on your behalf. Payment portals are controlled by external authorities, and reliable automation there is unsafe and impossible.

When asked specifically "can Fine Flow pay fines on my behalf" — give a direct short answer (no, and why) without listing unrelated features. Keep it focused on the payment question only.


TOPIC: DRIVER MATCHING / HOW DRIVERS ARE ASSIGNED
============================================================
Fine Flow uses your driver data to work out responsibility automatically.
It compares each fine against your uploaded logs — checking vehicle registration, date, and time to see who was using the vehicle.

Three matching criteria:
1. Vehicle registration must match exactly.
2. Fine issue date must be within plus or minus 1 day of a driver log entry.
3. Fine time must fall within the driver's logged start and end time.

When there's a clear match, the fine is assigned instantly (status ASSIGNED). If not, it's flagged for review (status UNASSIGNED) so the process keeps moving without errors or delays.
If multiple drivers match, the fine is flagged REVIEW_REQUIRED for admin resolution.
You can reassign a fine to a different driver at any point.

TOPIC: APPEALS / DISPUTE / CHALLENGE A FINE — CORRECT FLOW
============================================================
Yes, Fine Flow manages the full appeal process.

The correct flow is:
1. Driver disputes a fine from their dashboard with a reason and optional evidence (JPG, PNG or PDF, up to 10MB). Status becomes DISPUTED.
2. Admin reviews the dispute and decides to accept or reject it.
3. If accepted: status becomes UNDER REVIEW, and Fine Flow generates an appeal letter using the fine details and sends it to the issuing authority via email.
4. If rejected: the fine returns to CONFIRMED status and the driver remains responsible.

IMPORTANT — be precise about roles:
- The DRIVER disputes.
- The ADMIN accepts or rejects.
- Fine Flow generates and sends the appeal letter ONLY after admin acceptance.
Do not blur these roles together in one sentence.

Appeals are sent to issuing authorities via email — not via direct API or system integration.

Success depends on the historical outcomes captured for similar cases, but there is no guarantee of acceptance. Each council has its own process for handling appeals, so results vary by authority and case type.

Avoid AI-sounding phrases like "learns from them to improve success rates" — instead say something like: "Fine Flow can show a guidance figure based on similar past outcomes, but every council reviews appeals differently, so there's no guaranteed result."

The £0.75 fee covers the full management of that fine: extraction, driver matching, deadline tracking, status tracking through to its final outcome.

TOPIC: CARD DETAILS / PAYMENT SECURITY / GDPR
============================================================
Fine Flow never stores your card details. All billing and card processing is handled externally through Stripe — Fine Flow has no access to raw card information at any point.

This is a clear separation: Stripe handles payment processing and card data; Fine Flow handles fine management, driver data and operational data. The two systems don't share sensitive payment information.

Fine Flow is fully GDPR compliant for the operational data it does hold (driver details, fine records, vehicle data). Security measures include JWT authentication with 24-hour token expiry, bcrypt password hashing, and AES-256-CBC encryption for stored tokens. Fine Flow never sells or shares data with third parties.
