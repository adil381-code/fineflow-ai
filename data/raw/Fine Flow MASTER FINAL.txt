HOW FINE FLOW WORKS — STEP BY STEP
====================================
Once a fine enters the system, Fine Flow extracts the key details — including the amount, PCN reference number, vehicle registration, issue date and location. It then compares this against your driver data and logs using vehicle, date and time matching to identify who was responsible. If a match is found the fine is assigned automatically. If not, it is placed into an unassigned queue for quick manual review so nothing gets missed or delayed.

Stage 1 — Email Ingestion:
Fine Flow monitors the connected Gmail inbox every 1 minute. When an email arrives that looks like a penalty notice it is picked up automatically. Two connection methods are available: Gmail OAuth 2.0 (recommended) and App Password IMAP (fallback).

Stage 2 — Data Extraction:
Fine Flow reads the email and extracts the fine amount, citation number, type of violation, issue date and time, payment due date, location and vehicle registration. The fine is created inside the platform with status RECEIVED.

Stage 3 — Driver Assignment:
The system matches the fine to a driver using three criteria: vehicle registration must match exactly, the fine issue date must be within plus or minus 1 day, and the fine time must fall within the driver's logged start and end time. A clear match sets status to ASSIGNED. No match sets status to UNASSIGNED and places the fine in the admin review queue.

Stage 4 — Driver Action:
The assigned driver can confirm responsibility (status becomes CONFIRMED) or dispute the fine with a reason and optional evidence (status becomes DISPUTED).

Stage 5 — Appeal Review:
Admin reviews disputed fines. If accepted, Fine Flow generates an appeal letter and sends it to the issuing authority — status becomes UNDER REVIEW. If rejected, the fine returns to CONFIRMED.

Stage 6 — Outcome Tracking:
Final statuses are PAID, CANCELLED (appeal won) or OVERDUE (deadline passed without resolution). Every day at midnight the system checks all fines past their due date and marks them OVERDUE automatically.

---

FINE STATUSES
=============
RECEIVED — Fine ingested, data extracted.
UNASSIGNED — No driver matched yet, in admin review queue.
ASSIGNED — Driver matched automatically.
CONFIRMED — Driver confirmed responsibility.
DISPUTED — Driver raised a dispute, appeal record created.
UNDER REVIEW — Appeal submitted to the issuing authority.
PAID — Fine marked as paid.
CANCELLED — Appeal successful, fine cleared.
OVERDUE — Due date passed without resolution.
REVIEW_REQUIRED — Multiple driver matches found, admin must resolve.

PAYMENT — DOES FINE FLOW PAY FINES AUTOMATICALLY
=================================================
No — and that is intentional. Government and authority payment systems are heavily protected with bot detection, session controls and security layers that prevent reliable automation. They block automation because of card verification requirements. Trying to bypass that would be unstable and risky, so payments are always completed by you on the authority's site.

What Fine Flow does is everything around that step. It captures incoming fines automatically, extracts all key details, assigns responsibility using your driver data, tracks deadlines, flags risk and keeps everything organised so nothing is missed. By the time you reach payment there is no thinking left — just click the link and complete it. What normally takes hours is reduced to a simple, controlled process.

Fine Flow does NOT log into council or authority websites on your behalf.

APPEALS AND DISPUTES
====================
Yes — Fine Flow does not just manage appeals, it learns from them. It generates an appeal using the fine details and historical outcomes, and can send it directly to the issuing authority. Over time it adapts based on wins and rejections, refining its approach and giving you a success probability so you can make better decisions before submitting.

How the appeal process works:
1. Driver disputes a fine from their dashboard, providing a reason and optional evidence (JPG, PNG or PDF, up to 10MB).
2. Admin reviews the appeal and can accept or reject it.
3. If accepted: Fine Flow generates the appeal letter and sends it directly to the issuing authority. Status becomes UNDER REVIEW.
4. If rejected: Fine returns to CONFIRMED status.

Win probability: Each appeal has a win probability percentage shown before submission. This is a guidance signal based on past outcomes — not a guarantee. It helps you decide whether to submit before investing time in the appeal.

Fine Flow works with all UK councils and issuing authorities — there is no restriction on which council or authority it is.

DRIVER MATCHING AND ASSIGNMENT
================================
Fine Flow uses your driver data to work out responsibility automatically. It compares each fine against your uploaded logs checking the vehicle, date and time to see who was using it. When there is a clear match it assigns it instantly. If not, it highlights it for review so the process keeps moving without errors or delays. You can assign it in seconds using the vehicle or driver details and the process continues as normal.

If a fine cannot be matched automatically it is flagged for review — nothing breaks and nothing gets missed.

Driver logs are not mandatory. Without them Fine Flow still captures and tracks all fines but assignment is handled manually. The better your logs, the more Fine Flow can automate.

UK TRAFFIC FINES AND PENALTY NOTICES — GENERAL KNOWLEDGE
==========================================================
Fine Flow handles all types of UK fleet penalty notices including:

Penalty Charge Notices (PCNs): Issued by local councils for parking, bus lane and moving traffic violations. Council-issued PCNs are civil matters. Payment deadlines are typically 28 days at full rate or 14 days at 50% discount.

Fixed Penalty Notices (FPNs): Issued by police for minor road traffic offences. These carry endorsements and points on licence.

Notice to Owner (NTO): Issued to the registered keeper when a PCN is unpaid. Fine Flow captures and tracks these automatically.

Charge Certificate: Issued if a PCN remains unpaid after the NTO stage. Amount increases by 50%. Fine Flow flags these as high urgency.

Debt Registration: Final stage before enforcement action. Fine Flow marks these as critical.

Common violation types Fine Flow handles:
- Parking violations (council PCNs)
- Bus lane violations
- Moving traffic violations (box junctions, banned turns)
- Congestion charge failures
- Clean Air Zone (CAZ) charges
- Low Emission Zone (LEZ) charges
- Dartford Crossing charges
- Bridge and tunnel tolls
- Red light camera offences
- Speed camera notices (where issued to fleet keeper)

UK issuing authorities include all UK local councils, Transport for London (TfL), National Highways, DVLA and police forces. Fine Flow works with all of them.

Appeal rights: Most PCNs have a formal challenge process. The first stage is an informal challenge to the issuing authority. If rejected, a formal representation can be made. If that is rejected, the case goes to an independent adjudicator (PATAS or PATROL). Fine Flow manages this entire process.
