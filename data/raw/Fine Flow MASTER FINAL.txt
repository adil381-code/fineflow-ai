HOW FINE FLOW WORKS — STEP BY STEP
====================================

Q: How does it work? / Walk me through the process / Step by step
A: Once a fine arrives in your connected inbox, Fine Flow picks it up automatically. It extracts the key details — amount, PCN reference number, vehicle registration, issue date and location. It then compares this against your driver data and logs using vehicle, date and time matching to identify who was responsible. If a match is found the fine is assigned automatically. If not, it is placed into an unassigned queue for quick manual review so nothing gets missed or delayed.

Stage 1 — Email Ingestion: Fine Flow monitors the connected Gmail inbox every 1 minute. When an email arrives that looks like a penalty notice it is picked up automatically.

Stage 2 — Data Extraction: Fine Flow reads the email and extracts fine amount, citation number, violation type, issue date and time, payment due date, location and vehicle registration. The fine is created with status RECEIVED.

Stage 3 — Driver Assignment: The system matches the fine to a driver using three criteria: vehicle registration must match exactly, fine issue date must be within plus or minus 1 day, and fine time must fall within the driver's logged start and end time. A clear match sets status to ASSIGNED. No match sets status to UNASSIGNED and places the fine in the admin review queue.

Stage 4 — Driver Action: The assigned driver can confirm responsibility (status becomes CONFIRMED) or dispute the fine with a reason and optional evidence (status becomes DISPUTED).

Stage 5 — Appeal Review: Admin reviews disputed fines. If accepted, Fine Flow generates an appeal letter and sends it to the issuing authority by email — status becomes UNDER REVIEW. If rejected, fine returns to CONFIRMED.

Stage 6 — Outcome Tracking: Final statuses are PAID, CANCELLED (appeal won) or OVERDUE (deadline passed without resolution). Every day at midnight the system checks all fines past their due date and marks them OVERDUE automatically.

---

FINE STATUSES
=============

RECEIVED — Fine ingested from email, data extracted.
UNASSIGNED — No driver matched yet, placed in admin review queue.
ASSIGNED — Driver matched automatically.
CONFIRMED — Driver confirmed responsibility.
DISPUTED — Driver raised a dispute, appeal record created. This is a driver action.
UNDER REVIEW — Appeal submitted to issuing authority. This only happens AFTER admin accepts the dispute and submits the appeal.
PAID — Fine marked as paid.
CANCELLED — Appeal successful, fine cleared.
OVERDUE — Due date passed without resolution. System marks this automatically at midnight.
REVIEW_REQUIRED — Multiple driver matches found, admin must resolve manually.

PAYMENT — DOES FINE FLOW PAY FINES
====================================

Q: Does Fine Flow pay fines automatically? / Can Fine Flow pay fines on my behalf?
A: No — and that's intentional. Government and authority payment systems are heavily protected with bot detection, session controls, and security layers that prevent reliable automation. They all block automation because of card verification requirements. Trying to bypass that would be unstable and risky, so payments are always completed by you on the authority's site — they have to be.

What Fine Flow does is everything around that step. It captures incoming fines automatically, extracts all key details, assigns responsibility using your driver data, tracks deadlines, flags risk, and keeps everything organised so nothing is missed. Fines are grouped, prioritised, and ready to process, so you can handle multiple fines in one go without switching between systems. By the time you reach payment, there's no thinking left — just click the link and complete it.

Fine Flow does NOT log into council or authority websites on your behalf.

APPEALS AND DISPUTES
====================

Q: Can I appeal a fine with Fine Flow? / How do appeals work?
A: Yes — Fine Flow doesn't just manage appeals, it learns from them. Here is the process:

1. The driver disputes a fine from their dashboard, providing a reason and optional evidence (JPG, PNG or PDF, up to 10MB). Status becomes DISPUTED.
2. The admin reviews the appeal and can accept or reject it.
3. If accepted: Fine Flow generates an appeal letter using the fine details and historical outcomes, and sends it directly to the issuing authority by email. Status becomes UNDER REVIEW.
4. If rejected: Fine returns to CONFIRMED status and the driver must pay.

Appeals are sent to issuing authorities via email. Success depends on historical outcomes — there is no guarantee of universal acceptance. Each council has its own process for handling appeals, so results can vary depending on the authority and case type.

Fine Flow tracks outcomes from every appeal submitted. Over time it uses this history to improve future recommendations and generate stronger appeal letters based on what actually works.

Win probability: Each appeal shows a win probability percentage before submission. This is a guidance signal based on past outcomes — not a guarantee. It helps you decide whether to submit.

Q: Does Fine Flow support appeals for all councils?
A: Fine Flow sends appeal letters to all UK issuing authorities by email. However, each council has its own process for handling appeals, and success depends on the case and the authority. There is no guarantee that every council will accept or process appeals the same way. Fine Flow gives you the best possible chance by generating strong, evidence-based appeal letters.

UK TRAFFIC FINES — GENERAL KNOWLEDGE
======================================

Fine Flow handles all types of UK fleet penalty notices. Here is what fleet managers need to know:

Penalty Charge Notices (PCNs): Issued by local councils for parking, bus lane and moving traffic violations. Civil matters. Payment deadlines: 28 days at full rate, or 14 days at 50% discount. Fine Flow captures and tracks these automatically.

Fixed Penalty Notices (FPNs): Issued by police for minor road traffic offences. Carry endorsements and points on licence.

Notice to Owner (NTO): Issued to the registered keeper when a PCN is unpaid. Fine Flow tracks these as escalated fines.

Common violation types Fine Flow handles:
- Parking violations (council PCNs)
- Bus lane violations
- Moving traffic violations (box junctions, banned turns)
- Congestion charge failures
- Clean Air Zone (CAZ) and Low Emission Zone (LEZ) charges
- Dartford Crossing and toll charges
- Red light camera offences
- Speed camera notices issued to fleet keepers

UK issuing authorities: All UK local councils, Transport for London (TfL), National Highways, DVLA and police forces.

Appeal rights under UK law: Most PCNs have a formal challenge process. Stage 1 is an informal challenge to the authority. If rejected, a formal representation can be made. If that is rejected, the case goes to an independent adjudicator (PATAS in London, PATROL outside London). Fine Flow manages this entire process.
