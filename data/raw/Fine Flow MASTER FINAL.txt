FineFlow
Complete System Flow & Feature Overview

This document covers everything that is built and live in FineFlow — from how fines enter the system to how billing works, how drivers are managed, and how the referral system operates.

1. What Is FineFlow

FineFlow is an automated UK fleet fine (PCN) management platform. Fleet companies receive parking fines constantly and FineFlow handles the entire lifecycle — from reading the fine email, to deciding what to do with it, to managing appeals and logging outcomes — so the fleet manager does not have to do any of it manually.

Key billing rules:

- Credits reset to zero at the start of each new billing cycle
- When credits run out, each additional fine is charged at £2.00 overage rate
- There is also a £10.00 per vehicle charge if the vehicle limit is exceeded
- Overage charges are added to the outstanding balance and collected at the next billing cycle
- A company cannot resubscribe if they have an outstanding balance — it must be cleared first
- If a company cancels, a cooldown period applies until the end of their current billing period — they cannot resubscribe during this time
- Credits do not roll over to the next month — unused credits are forfeited

3. Fine Lifecycle — From Email to Resolution

This is the core of FineFlow. Every fine goes through the following stages from the moment it arrives to the moment it is resolved.

Stage 1 — Email Ingestion

FineFlow monitors the connected Gmail inbox every 1 minute. When an email arrives with keywords like fine, penalty, violation, or challan in the subject line, the system picks it up automatically. Two connection methods are supported — Gmail OAuth 2.0 (primary) and App Password IMAP (fallback).

Stage 2 — Data Extraction

The system uses Groq LLaMA 3.1 AI to read the email and extract the following 9 fields from the email text:

- Fine amount and currency
- Citation number (reference number)
- Type of violation
- Issue date and time
- Payment due date
- Location of violation
- Vehicle registration number

Once extracted the fine is created in the system with status RECEIVED and one credit is deducted from the company's balance.

Stage 3 — Driver Assignment

The system attempts to automatically match the fine to a driver based on three criteria:

1. Vehicle registration must match exactly
2. Fine issue date must be within plus or minus 1 day of a driver log entry
3. Fine time must fall within the driver log start and end time

If a match is found the fine status changes to ASSIGNED. If no match is found it remains UNASSIGNED and sits in the admin queue for manual review.

Stage 4 — Fine Statuses

Every fine in the system has one of the following statuses at any given time:

Status: RECEIVED
What It Means: Fine has been ingested from email, data extracted, credit deducted

Status: UNASSIGNED
What It Means: Fine created but no driver matched yet

Status: ASSIGNED
What It Means: Driver has been matched to the fine

Status: CONFIRMED
What It Means: Driver has confirmed they were responsible

Status: DISPUTED
What It Means: Driver has filed an appeal

Status: PAID
What It Means: Fine has been marked as paid

Status: OVERDUE
What It Means: Due date has passed without resolution

Status: CANCELLED
What It Means: Appeal was successful, fine cancelled

Stage 5 — Driver Action

Once a fine is assigned to a driver, the driver can take one of two actions from their dashboard:

- Confirm responsibility — fine status moves to CONFIRMED
- Dispute the fine — driver provides a reason and optional evidence, fine status moves to DISPUTED and an appeal record is created

Stage 6 — Appeal Review

When a driver disputes a fine, the company admin reviews the appeal. The admin can:

- Accept the appeal — an email is sent to the authority on behalf of the company, appeal status moves to UNDER REVIEW
- Reject the appeal — fine status moves back to CONFIRMED and the driver must pay

Evidence can be uploaded by the driver in JPG, PNG, or PDF format up to 10MB. The appeal outcome is tracked and reported in the appeals dashboard.

Stage 7 — Overdue Scheduler

Every day at midnight the system automatically checks all fines where the due date has passed. Any fine with status RECEIVED, UNASSIGNED, ASSIGNED, or CONFIRMED that is past its due date is automatically updated to OVERDUE. PAID, CANCELLED, and DISPUTED fines are not affected.

4. Gmail Connection

FineFlow supports two methods for connecting a company's Gmail inbox to receive fine emails.

Method: Gmail OAuth 2.0
How It Works: User clicks Connect Gmail, redirected to Google login, approves access there. No password stored.
Security: Tokens encrypted with AES-256-CBC. Auto-refreshes 5 minutes before expiry.
Status: Primary method

Method: App Password
How It Works: User generates a 16-character app password inside Google and pastes it into FineFlow.
Security: Password encrypted before storage.
Status: Fallback method

The system checks Gmail OAuth first. If no OAuth tokens exist it falls back to App Password. Gmail OAuth is the recommended method going forward.

5. Driver Management

Adding Drivers

- Drivers can be added individually via the dashboard
- Bulk import via CSV upload is supported

Required CSV columns: drivername, driveremail, driverphone, licensenumber, isactive. Optional columns: assignedvehicle, username, password. If no password is provided the system generates one automatically and emails the credentials to the driver.

Driver Dashboard

Each driver has their own login and can see:

- Total fines received
- Fines pending their confirmation
- Points on licence (count of all fines excluding cancelled and disputed)
- Total amount paid
- Appeals currently in progress
- Safe driving streak (days since last fine)
- 12-month fine history chart

Driver Tracking

The system tracks each driver's complete fine history, licence points, and driving streak. This data is visible to both the driver and the company admin.

6. Dashboards

Admin Dashboard (Internal Use Only)

The admin dashboard is for FineFlow's internal team only. It shows:

- Total active client companies
- Monthly and annual revenue
- Total fines processed across all clients
- Total registered drivers across all companies
- Total appeals won system-wide
- System-wide success rate
- Full list of all companies with their plan, subscription status, fleet size, and contact details

Company Dashboard (Client Facing)

Each client company has their own dashboard showing:

- Fines processed this year
- Estimated admin cost saved
- Time saved by using FineFlow
- Appeals won
- Credit balance, credit limit, credits used
- Current subscription plan and status
- Vehicle count against vehicle limit
- Outstanding balance and next billing date
- Processing costs breakdown

Driver Dashboard

Each driver has a personal dashboard showing their own fine history, points, appeals, and driving record as described in Section 5.

7. User Roles & Access

Role: Admin
What They Can Do: Full access to all companies and system metrics. Can create and manage admins. Can delete companies. Internal use only.

Role: Company
What They Can Do: Manage own company drivers, fines, appeals, billing, and reports. Limited to their own company data only.

Role: Driver
What They Can Do: View personal fines only. Confirm or dispute fines assigned to them. Access personal dashboard. Cannot see other drivers.

11. Security & Authentication

- All users authenticated via JWT tokens with a 24-hour expiry
- Passwords hashed with bcrypt at 10 salt rounds
- Gmail tokens and app passwords encrypted with AES-256-CBC before storage
- Forgot password flow uses a secure one-time token with 1-hour expiry
- All routes protected with role-based middleware — drivers cannot access company routes and companies cannot access admin routes
- All data is GDPR compliant — no data is sold or shared with third parties
